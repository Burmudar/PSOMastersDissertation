Scout Bee

The artificial bees are modelled on the behaviour of real bees. Thus an employed bee can also abandon certain food sources when it has outlived its usefulness. Abandonment of a food source can occur for the following reasons[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###]:
  • The employed bee has reached the maximum allowed cycles to improve the nectar amount. The maximum cycles spent on a food source allow the algorithm to avoid local optima[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###].
  • The bee cannot improve the search represented by the food source any further[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###].
When a food source in the algorithm is abandoned, it needs to be replaced by a new food source[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###]. Note that a food source is not abandoned when it represents the globally best found solution. An employed bee transitions to a new role when it abandons a food source from an employed bee to a scout bee[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###].

It is the responsibility of the scout bee to replace the abandoned food source with a new randomly generated one[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCImageEnhancement##1###]. In research done by Gómez-Iglesias et al. [#ABCFusionGrid##1###] an extension is made to the scout bees. The scout bee individuals are divided into two types of bees, namely rovers and cubs bees[#ABCFusionGrid##1###].

  • <#4264#>Rover bees<#4264#> are similar to traditional scout bees and hence use diversification strategies to explore the search space.
  • <#4265#>Cub bees<#4265#> explore the search space relative to a good solution found by a rover by randomly changing configuration parameters.
By using two different scout bees a good balance is achieved when searching the search space in the beginning where diversity is preferred and late in the algorithm where intensification is preferred [#ABCFusionGrid##1###].
