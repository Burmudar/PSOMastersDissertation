Mobile Station

A <#570#>MS<#570#> as it is defined in the <#571#>gsm<#571#> specification refers to any mobile device that is capable of making and receiving calls on a <#572#>gsm<#572#> network. The <#573#>MS<#573#> is the main gateway for a user to gain access to the <#574#>gsm<#574#> network [#Eisenblatter##1###,#GSMArchitectureProtocolsServices##1###]. Typical devices that fall under the category of <#576#>MS<#576#> are cellular phones, smart phones and <#577#>POS<#577#> devices. The <#578#>MS<#578#> has two features that play an important role throughout the <#579#>gsm<#579#> network, namely:
<#14870#><#581#>SIM<#581#><#14870#>
--- Inserted into a mobile device. The <#582#>SIM<#582#> contains the <#583#>IMSI<#583#> and is used throughout the network for authentication as well as being a key part in providing encrypted transmissions [#Eisenblatter##1###].
<#14871#><#585#>IMEI<#585#><#14871#>
--- Used to identify mobile station equipment. Primarily used in the denial of service to equipment that has been blacklisted2.3 and tries to gain access to the network [#Eisenblatter##1###].

The <#688#>MS<#688#> has the capability to change the transmission power it uses from 0.8, 2, 5, 8 MW to a maximum value of 20 MW[#GSMSysEngin##1###]. The change in transmission power is automatically set to the lowest level by the <#690#>BTS<#690#> to ensure reliable communication after evaluating the signal strength as measured by the <#691#>MS<#691#> [#GSMSysEngin##1###,#GSMArchitectureProtocolsServices##1###]. The power adjustment also minimises co-channel interference because the transmitted signal is less powerful and therefore less likely to interfere with other signals[#GSMSysEngin##1###].
