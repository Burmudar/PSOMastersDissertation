Employed and Onlooker Bees

As previously outlined, when recruited onlooker bees reach the advertised food source that is stored in memory, they do not occupy the same food source[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###]. Instead the bees explore the immediate neighbourhood of the food source that was communicated to them[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCFusionGrid##1###,#ABCReconfigDistro##1###]. They seek to find a food source that improves on the previous one[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###]. After a new solution #tex2html_wrap_inline15477# mathend000# is produced, the bee takes the new and old solutions from memory to compare their respective nectar contents. If the new solution is found to have higher quality nectar, the bee replaces the old solution in memory with the new solution[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCReconfigDistro##1###]. Otherwise, the bee abandons the new solution and keeps the old solution in memory[#ABCCompareStudy##1###,#ABCNumericalOptimization##1###]. Thus, the bee seeks to always move towards a better solution and therefore uses a greedy selection process[#ABCLeafConstrained##1###,#ABCReconfigDistro##1###].

One of the problems with the above approach is that little information about the food source is used in generating a neighbouring food source. In the research by Singh [#ABCLeafConstrained##1###] a slight variation is proposed to generating food source neighbours by using more global information. The author adds a constraint to the algorithm that all neighbouring solutions generated by employed bees must be unique.

When an employed bee generates a neighbour and an identical solution already exists in the system, a collision is said to have occurred. A collision is solved by letting the employed bee transition to a scout bee so that a completely random solution can be generated [#ABCLeafConstrained##1###]. Scout and onlooker bee generated solutions are not checked if they collide with other solutions in the system since the aim for them is exploring and not exploiting as with employed bees[#BeeJobShop##1###,#ABCCompareStudy##1###].
