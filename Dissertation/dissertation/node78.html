Tabu Search on the FAP

In a study conducted by Robert Montemanni and Derek Smith [#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###] the <#2620#>TS<#2620#> algorithm is used on the <#2621#>FS-FAP<#2621#>. The authors had to make some alterations to the algorithm to suit their needs as well as to make the algorithm more efficient in exploring in the <#2622#>FAP<#2622#> solution space.

The <#2623#>TS<#2623#> algorithm used by the authors is the multistart <#2624#>TS<#2624#> algorithm, which randomly starts on different initial solutions [#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###].

The authors developed a technique called <#2626#>HMT<#2626#>. <#2627#>HMT<#2627#> first monitors an underlying heuristic being used on the problem by the algorithm[#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###]. It then identifies certain characteristics that good solutions exhibit. In the <#2629#>FAP<#2629#>, it is transmitters that are assigned different frequencies which results in an overall lower interference value[#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###].

The HMT then uses the identified characteristics to add additional constraints to the problem[#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###]. By adding constraints, the search space is reduced. However, by reducing the search space, other near-optimal solutions which might be far better are excluded[#TabuMontemanniSmith##1###]. It is for this reason that that Montemanni and Smith opted not to add the constraints permanently.

Montemanni and Smith applied their <#2634#>TS<#2634#> algorithm together with HMT to the <#2635#>COST<#2635#> 259 family of benchmarks, specifically the Siemens1, Siemens2, Siemens3 and Siemens4 problems. The results are presented in table~#TSCOST259#2636>. The values presented are scalar and indicate the total interference of the frequency plan. The lower the interference value is, the better the frequency plan.
