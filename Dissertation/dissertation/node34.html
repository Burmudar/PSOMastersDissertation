GSM Interfaces

In the <#924#>gsm<#924#> network all the network components communicate with each other through predefined interfaces. In this section an overview is given of these interfaces between the components.
<#983#>Um interface<#983#> --- This interface is the link between an <#927#>MS<#927#> device and a <#928#>BTS<#928#> and is also referred to as the Air interface since communication occurs wirelessly. The primary protocol used on this interface is the <#930#>LAPDm<#930#>, which is an extension of the <#931#>ISDN<#931#> LAPD protocol to accommodate the mobile nature of <#932#>MS<#932#> devices as well as for the shorter <#933#>TDMA<#933#> frames which are used in <#934#>gsm<#934#> networks[#wirelesstelcoMullet##1###,#GSMSecurInTeleNetwork##1###].
<#984#>Abis interface<#984#> --- Between the <#937#>BTS<#937#> and <#938#>BSC<#938#> the interface used for communication is known as the Abis interface. The only messages that the <#939#>BTS<#939#> is interested in are those that have to do with management of radio resources[#wirelesstelcoMullet##1###,#GSMSecurInTeleNetwork##1###]. All other messages are left alone and merely pass through the <#941#>BTS<#941#> to the <#942#>BSC<#942#> transparently.
<#985#>A interface<#985#> --- The interface between the <#944#>BSC<#944#> and <#945#>MSC<#945#> is known as the A interface. This interface is used for the transfer of information, which is used by the <#946#>MSC<#946#> to manage BSSs, control connections and manage the mobility of <#947#>MS<#947#> in its area of service[#wirelesstelcoMullet##1###,#GSMArchitectureProtocolsServices##1###].
<#986#>Other Interfaces<#986#> --- The <#950#>MSC<#950#> has interfaces going from itself to databases and other external networks. Each interface connects to a specific database, <#951#>MSC<#951#> or network and is therefore very specific as to what function it performs[#wirelesstelcoMullet##1###,#GSMArchitectureProtocolsServices##1###]. An interface going from one <#953#>MSC<#953#> to another will convey information regarding handling the administration of handover of an <#954#>MS<#954#> device leaving one administrative area to another MSC's administrative area. The administrative area of an Msc is the section of <#955#>BSS<#955#> components the Msc is responsible for. The handover procedure is a very delicate process which is described in section~#sec:handover#956>.
