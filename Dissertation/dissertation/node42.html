Frequency Allocation Types

In this section the different methods used to allocate frequencies to cells in a cellular network are discussed. Furthermore the method that relates to the specific <#1561#>FAP<#1561#> variant in this dissertation is described.

Within the <#1562#>FAP<#1562#> domain there are different types of <#1563#>FAP<#1563#>, which have emerged over the years as the domain of wireless communications matured and technological requirements changed. These <#1564#>FAP<#1564#> variants are discussed in section~#sec:FAPVariants#1565>.

There are a variety of <#1566#>FAP<#1566#>s in the wireless communication domain but each individual problem can be classified into one of the following two categories based on the way frequencies are assigned to cells:

  • <#1568#>FFA<#1568#> is where frequencies assigned to cells are static; therefore they cannot be changed until a new assignment plan is calculated[#PrinciplesMobileCommunication##1###].
  • <#1570#>DFA<#1570#> is the process of allocating frequencies to cells as required to meet the current traffic demand imposed on them by clients[#PrinciplesMobileCommunication##1###].
